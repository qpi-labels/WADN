Why WADN?

In 2034, the formation of NPNU posed a threat to global democracy.
WADN was established to protect democracy in a rapidly changing society
and address emerging social issues.
















Society

WADN provides humanitarian aid such as vaccines, medicine, and rescue workers
in urgent situations like disasters, crisis areas, or for continuous support needed due to environmental pollution or endemic diseases.
We also strive to offer democratic solutions to emerging societal problems
based on human rights.




Economy

WADN supports free trade activities among member states based on capitalism.
We provide continuous aid to reduce development gaps for developing member states,
supporting their economic growth and strengthening economic cooperation.




Military

WADN strongly condemns military interventions that suppress democracy
and provides military aid and humanitarian assistance to victims
when member states face military aggression threatening their democracy.
